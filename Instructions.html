Instructions for Flower Skull

Overview

Flower Skull is played in rounds. The game ends when any player wins two rounds.

Each round has four phases: Set Up, Playing Cards, Bidding, and Followthrough.

Phase 1: Set Up

At the beginning of the game all players will have a hand of three flowers and one skull.

Each round starts off with each player laying down one card, beginning their personal stack.

Whenever you play a card, it will be unknown to your opponents.

Phase 2: Playing Cards

In this phase of the game you will choose between playing a card, or starting the bidding phase.

If you play a card, you will place it on top of your stack

If you choose to begin bidding, you will not play a card

Phase 3: Bidding

In this phase of the game you will bid on how many flower cards you can uncover before uncovering a skull from the cards that have been played.

On your turn you must either bid higher than the previous player, or drop out of the round.

Once one player remains in the bid they will continue to the followthrough phase.

Phase 4: Followthrough

In this phase you (who won the bid) will now attempt to reveal the number of flower cards you bid without overturning a skull card

If you flip over a skull card, you have lost the round. As a consequence you will discard a card from your hand.

You will begin by revealing the cards from your stack, one at a time.

If you have flipped all the cards from your stack and have not met your bid, you will now begin revealing cards from your opponent's stacks

You may choose any of your opponent's stacks and alternate between them, but you can only ever flip the top card of their stack.

If you meet your bid, you have won the round! Congratulations

End Game

Again, a player wins the game after winning two rounds.
If a player loses a round (i.e. wins the bidding but cannot meet their bid) they discard a card for the remainder of the game
